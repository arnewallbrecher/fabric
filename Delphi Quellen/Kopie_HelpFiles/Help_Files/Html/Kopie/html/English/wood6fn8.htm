Distributions between the isotropic case and clusters lie on the ordinate. Distributions between the isotropic case and great circle ditributions are on the abscissa. Since the degree of preferred orientation increases with the distance from the zero point, the ratio


is a measure for preferred orientation.

The table on the right side shows the c-values calculated from the eigenvalues. The c-value lies between 1 (isotropic) and (max. degree of preferred orientation.

In the menus 'colour' and 'symbols' the colour and symbol of the plotted points can be changed.
