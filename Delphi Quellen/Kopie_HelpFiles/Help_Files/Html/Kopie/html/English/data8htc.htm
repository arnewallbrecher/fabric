Data Points from a Map

The programm finds the geographic, Gauss-Krueger, or UTM coordinates of a point, indicated in the map. The map must already exist as a '*.bmp' or '*.jpg' file, or on the clipboard. It can then be imported. The margins of the map are typed into the relevant fields either as geograhic, Gauss-Krueger, or UTM-coordinates. If data points are marked on the backside of a map, the map is drawn back to front.

Procedure:

1) Decide in the menu 'import map', whether the map is imported from a data carrier or from the clipboard.

2) Indicate in the menu 'marking of data locations', whether the map is drawn with the front side or back to front.

3) Type the coordinates of the margins in the relevant fields.

4) Click on the data points.

5) Mark the fabric type with 'p' for planes or 'l' for linears, type azimuth and dip angle.

6) Click on 'terminate input' when all points are clicked.

7) Create the map by clicking on 'plot'.
