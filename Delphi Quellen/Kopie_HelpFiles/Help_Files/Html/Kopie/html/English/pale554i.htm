Dihedra Method

This method is used to determine the directions of the three principal stresses (σ1, σ2, σ3) from slickenside planes and striations.


The slickenside plane is drawn as a great circle into a projection. Then a great circle is drawn normal to the striation (auxiliary plane). These two planes divide the globe into an extensional and a compressional dihedron.

If compressional and extensional dihedra of several displacement data are superposed, the intersection of compressional dihedra contains s1, and the intersection of the extensional dihedra contains s3
