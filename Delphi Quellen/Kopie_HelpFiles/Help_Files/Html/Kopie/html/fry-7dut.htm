Normalized Fry Method

In the normalized Fry method the distance between two particle centres (particles A and B) is divided by the sum of the two radii:

i.e., touching particles have the normalized distance

,

regardless of their sizes (Erslev, 1988). To apply this method, the input must contain the (x,y) coordinates of particle centres and the radii of the particles. When a polygon file is used, the radii are calculated automatically.

To make an input from an image you must first click on the centre and then on a point of the periphery of a particle. Normalized Fry plots show a much clearer area without points and a better concentration of points on the periphery of this area.
