Projection Method (Panozzo (1984), modified)

In an image of a grain fabric (thin section or polished surface), single grains are digitised as closed polygons. These polygons are rotated around 180°. During this rotation the sum of the lengths of the projections of grain boundaries on a reference line for each increment of rotation. (ΣP[α]) are measured. (Panozzo, 1984). ΣP[α]max is the long axis of sumarized strain ellipse. The corresponding angle Θ is the plunge of the ellipse The ratio ΣP[α]max / ΣP[α]min is the ellipticity (R) of the fabric (Fig. 1).
