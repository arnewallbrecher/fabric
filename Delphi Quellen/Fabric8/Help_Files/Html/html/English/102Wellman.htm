In this program the ellipticity of the finite strain-ellipse is found, together with its orientation towards a reference line. The construction of a finite strain-ellipse is done with former rectangular markers, like hinge lines and median lines of brachiopods. The resulting ellipse is similar to the true strain ellipse. From its long and short diameter the ellipticity is calculated. The method is explained in Fig. 1 and 2.

When you draw in undeformed  state parallel lines  to hinge and median line through the end of a fixed line, you receive rectangular triangles (1 to 3) lying on a Thales' circle. When you shear  the objects and the Thales' circle in one deformation, the points (1 to 3) ly on an ellipse  which is similar to the strain ellipse, i.e. its ellipticity and inclination are the same as those of strain ellipse. During evaluation you click on the endpoints of the markers. The programme then creates the parallel lines  through two points. You click on the intersection points of the parallels. From these points the ellipse is found and its ellipticity and inlination calculated.
