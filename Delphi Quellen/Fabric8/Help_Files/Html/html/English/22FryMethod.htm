Fry Method

This method can be applied to find the finite strain when an originally randomly distributed population of points on a surface is changed by flattening or shearing. The method will give good results for randomly distributed markers in a matrix of other material.  Completely grain supported fabrics are not suitable. The manual evaluation is done like this: The centre points are plotted on a paper. On a transparent paper a point is marked and put on one point of the paper (Fig. A). The procedure is repeated for all points (Fig. B). Each time that this point is put over a point of the original plot, all the other points are copied on the transparency.. The result is an elliptic area in the middle of the transparency with no points and a high concentration of points at the margin of this area. The shape of this area and its orientation resembles the shape of the strain ellipse (Fig. C). This procedure was programmed. The user must only create the coordinate matrix for the centre points of the markers.


A) The centre of the transparency (cross) is put on one point, all other points are are copied to the transparency  B) Put the cross on the second point and copy all other points. C) Final diagram
