Rf/φ Method, Results

In the Rf/Phi-diagram Rf is in logarithmic scale. From the symmetrie of the distribution the vectorial mean  of the Phi-values and the harmonic mean of the Rf-values is calculated (horizontal and vertical line). The symmetry index (0.97) is calculated from the number of points in each quadrant and compared with a table of values published by Lisle (1985) .

The symmetrie index ist: 

                                  Isym = 1 – (|nA – nB| + |nC – nD|) / N 

(A – D are the quadrants) 

A symmetrie index larger the the value of the table indicates that the distribution is probably symmetrical. A smaller value might indicate an initial preferred orientation. The probable Rs–value i.e. the ellipticity of the strain ellipse is given by the minimum of the Chi2 curve.  
                

The theory behind these values is described in detail by Lisle (1985).
