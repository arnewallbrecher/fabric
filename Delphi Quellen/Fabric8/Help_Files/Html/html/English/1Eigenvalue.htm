Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Eigenvectors of a matrix are those vectors, which, multiplied with the matrix, are scalar multiples of themselves.

.

λ is the eigenvalue.

Eigenvalues can be found by solving the equation

det(A - λ I) =0 (I = identity matrix).

The eigenvectors are then calculated from (A - λI) = 0.

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors of structural orientation data can be explained with a physical model.


Supposing the data are represented by mass points on the globe (with the masses m = 1), two axes must exist which have a maximum and a minimum moment of inertia. a third axis is normal to these two axes. With these three axes an ellipsoid can be constructed in analogy to the stress- or strain-ellipsoid. This ellipsoid is called the orientation-ellipsoid. the corresponding tensor is the orientation tensor
